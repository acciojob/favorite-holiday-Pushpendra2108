Mussoorie

Mussoorie, often called the "Queen of the Hills," is a charming hill station nestled in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, India. Perched at an altitude of approximately 2,000 meters (6,600 feet) above sea level, Mussoorie offers breathtaking views of the Shivalik ranges and the Doon Valley. The town's lush green hills, cascading waterfalls, and colonial-era architecture make it a popular getaway for nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike. With its pleasant weather throughout the year, Mussoorie is a perfect destination for a peaceful and rejuvenating vacation.


Top 5 attractions

  1. Kempty Falls: A cascading waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, offering a refreshing escape and thrilling water activities.
  2. Lal Tibba: The highest peak in Mussoorie, providing panoramic views of the Himalayas and the Doon Valley.
  3. Gun Hill: A historic point accessible by cable car, offering stunning views and a glimpse into the colonial past.
  4. Company Garden: A well-maintained botanical garden with a variety of flora, offering a serene escape amidst nature.
  5. Mall Road: The heart of Mussoorie, lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants, offering a vibrant shopping and dining experience.

Must try food

  • Aloo ke Gutke: A flavorful and crispy potato dish.
  • Kafuli: A traditional meat curry with unique spices.
  • Phannu: A local delicacy made from buckwheat flour.
  • Gahat ke Paranthe: A type of flatbread stuffed with lentil filling.
  • Jhangora ki Kheer: A sweet rice pudding made with millet.
